Emergency Dental Care

Dental Emergency?

We provide same-day emergency appointments

Call +1 610-365-3266

Dental emergencies can happen at any time and often require immediate attention to prevent further complications. Our experienced team is equipped to handle urgent dental situations with prompt, professional care. We understand that dental pain and emergencies can be distressing, which is why we prioritize emergency appointments and provide compassionate treatment when you need it most.

When to Seek Emergency Care

Not all dental issues require emergency treatment, but some situations need immediate attention to prevent permanent damage or severe complications. If you're experiencing severe pain, bleeding, swelling, or trauma to your teeth or mouth, don't wait ‚Äì contact us immediately.

Our emergency dental services are available to help you get relief from pain and address urgent dental problems. We strive to see emergency patients the same day whenever possible, ensuring you receive the care you need when you need it most.

Important: If you have a dental emergency outside of our regular business hours, please call our emergency line. For severe trauma involving facial bones or uncontrolled bleeding, go to the nearest emergency room immediately.

Common Dental Emergencies

Severe Toothache

Intense, persistent tooth pain that interferes with daily activities. Often indicates infection or deep decay requiring immediate treatment.

Knocked-Out Tooth

A completely dislodged tooth due to trauma. Time is critical ‚Äì the tooth may be saved if treated within 30 minutes to an hour.

Broken or Chipped Tooth

Fractured teeth from accidents or biting hard objects. Large breaks may expose nerves and cause severe pain.

Dental Abscess

Serious infection that can spread to other parts of the body. Symptoms include severe pain, swelling, and fever.

Lost Filling or Crown

Dental restorations that have fallen out, leaving the tooth vulnerable to damage and sensitivity.

Soft Tissue Injuries

Cuts, tears, or punctures to the tongue, cheeks, gums, or lips that may require immediate attention.

What to Do Before You Arrive

Taking the right steps immediately after a dental emergency can make a significant difference in the treatment outcome. Here are some first aid measures you can take while en route to our office.

For a Knocked-Out Tooth:

  1. Handle the tooth by the crown, not the root
  2. Rinse gently with water if dirty
  3. Try to reinsert the tooth in its socket
  4. If unable to reinsert, store in milk or saliva
  5. Get to the dentist within 30 minutes

For Severe Tooth Pain:

  1. Rinse mouth with warm salt water
  2. Use dental floss to remove trapped food
  3. Take over-the-counter pain medication
  4. Apply cold compress to outside of cheek
  5. Avoid placing aspirin directly on the gum

Remember: These are temporary measures to help manage pain and protect your teeth until you can receive professional treatment. Emergency dental care is essential for proper diagnosis and treatment of urgent dental problems.
