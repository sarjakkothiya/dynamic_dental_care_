Emergency Dentistry: Immediate Care When You Need It Most

Dental emergencies can be sudden, painful, and frightening. Knowing when to seek emergency dental care and what to expect can save your smile and prevent complications. At our clinic, we provide prompt and compassionate emergency dentistry to address urgent dental problems effectively.

What Qualifies as a Dental Emergency?

Not all dental issues require emergency care, but some conditions demand immediate attention to relieve pain, prevent infection, or save a tooth. Common dental emergencies include:

  • Severe toothache that won‚Äôt go away
  • Knocked-out (avulsed) tooth
  • Broken or fractured teeth
  • Lost dental fillings or crowns causing pain or sensitivity
  • Abscess or swelling indicating infection
  • Injury to gums, tongue, or other soft tissues
  • Uncontrolled bleeding inside the mouth

When Should You Visit Emergency Dentistry?

If you experience any of the above symptoms, do not delay seeking care. Early treatment often prevents worsening conditions and reduces the risk of tooth loss or serious infections. For less urgent dental problems, schedule a regular appointment.

Quick tip: If a tooth is knocked out, keep it moist by placing it in milk or saliva and get to the dentist immediately for the best chance of saving it.

Emergency Dentistry Treatments

Our emergency dental team is trained to diagnose and treat urgent problems efficiently. Some treatments you may receive include:

  • Pain relief through medication or root canal therapy
  • Re-implanting or stabilizing knocked-out or loosened teeth
  • Repairing broken teeth with bonding, crowns, or fillings
  • Draining abscesses and prescribing antibiotics
  • Controlling bleeding and treating soft tissue injuries

How to Prevent Dental Emergencies

While accidents happen, you can reduce the risk of dental emergencies by:

  • Wearing a mouthguard during sports or high-risk activities
  • Maintaining good oral hygiene to prevent decay and infection
  • Avoiding chewing hard objects like ice or popcorn kernels
  • Visiting your dentist regularly for checkups and early problem detection
  • Addressing dental problems promptly before they worsen
